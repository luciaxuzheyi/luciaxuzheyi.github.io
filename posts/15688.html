Oceanarium Photo Guide

There are some general guidelines and rules when photoing at ocean park.

  • Flash is not allowed in the aquarium, because it is bad for marine life.
  • Flash may also causes glass reflection (but the auxiliary indicator light of my Nikon always automatically turns on when focusing itemsüò≠There is no way, just block it with your finger)
  • High ISO is essential when photoing indoor, so remember to turn on high ISO noise reduction (but it is said that some cameras can't shoot continuously with the noise reduction function turned on?)
  • Glass reflection problem: use a hood or photo by sticking to the glass to reduce reflections
  • Aperture Priority S mode is on: if overexposure, reduce exposure compensation by 1

Photo marine life & divers staff

  • high-speed continuous shooting mode essential
  • the faster the shutter, the better (the s/ number should be as small as possible)
  • the larger the aperture, the better (the f/ number should be as large as possible)
